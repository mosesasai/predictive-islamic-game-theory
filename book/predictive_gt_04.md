## Source: Predictive History — Game Theory #4: The Immigration Trap

## 1. Current Problem Definition

**The Assimilation Paradox:** High-achieving immigrant groups (e.g., East Asians in Western countries) achieve economic success but lack proportional social status and reproductive success. Playing by host country rules guarantees loss in the status game. Groups refusing assimilation (maintaining high birth rates and internal cohesion) outcompete assimilators in the long run.

**Game Structure:**
- **Players:** Immigrant communities vs. host society establishment
- **Strategies:** Full assimilation vs. cultural preservation vs. parallel community building
- **Nash Equilibrium:** Assimilated immigrants reach economic ceiling; non-assimilating groups grow demographically and eventually dominate

---

## 2. Classical Game Theory Approach

Modeling immigration as a repeated coordination game:
- **Assimilation strategy:** Adopt host culture, compete in host institutions -> blocked by glass ceiling, dating market discrimination, institutional gatekeeping
- **Parallel community strategy:** Maintain own institutions, high internal cohesion, high birth rate -> grows in proportion over time
- **Nash prediction:** Assimilators lose; parallel communities grow; host society fractures along identity lines

This is structurally identical to the "World Game" (Speech 05): energy + cohesion + openness beats wealth + complacency + division.

## 5. Conclusion

The immigration trap is a game-theoretic inevitability under Nash logic: host societies gatekeep status, assimilators lose identity, and parallel communities fragment the society.
