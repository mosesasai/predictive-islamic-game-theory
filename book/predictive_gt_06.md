## 1. Current Problem Definition

**The Global Extraction Game:** The British Empire created the modern capitalist system through a mechanism of bribing foreign elites to loot their own countries, then laundering stolen wealth through offshore financial centers. This system — over-financialization — has now corrupted Western nations themselves, creating extreme inequality and unsustainable extraction heading toward collapse.

---

## 2. Classical Game Theory Approach

**Principal-Agent Problem at Global Scale:**
- **Imperial center (principal):** Maximizes extraction from periphery
- **Local elites (agents):** Bribed to facilitate extraction in exchange for personal enrichment
- **Local population:** Bears extraction cost with no voice

**Nash Equilibrium:** Imperial center and local elites collude against local population. Wealth flows outward through offshore centers. The system is individually rational for center and local elite but collectively catastrophic for periphery populations and (eventually) for the center itself as over-financialization hollows out productive capacity.

## 3. Islamic Game Theory Approach

1. **Riba Prohibition:** The offshore financial system is built entirely on compound interest, derivatives, and financial engineering. Prohibiting Riba eliminates the mathematical engine of extraction ($w(t) = w_0 e^{rt}$).

2. **Anti-Hoarding:** The Prophet prohibited hoarding (ihtikar) and made sitting on idle wealth costly through Zakat. Offshore wealth parking — the core mechanism of the extraction game — is structurally impossible under IGT.

3. **Amanah Constraint on Leaders:** In IGT, leaders face divine accountability for betraying their people's trust. The "bribed local elite" strategy becomes dominated when $\lambda \cdot \Omega(\text{betray people}) \ll 0$.

4. **Khalifah (Stewardship):** Resources belong to God; rulers are stewards, not owners. This eliminates the property rights framework that enables extraction.

## 5. Conclusion

The global financial system is a multi-level principal-agent extraction game whose Nash Equilibrium produces exactly the wealth concentration and social decay observed today. IGT provides the structural antidote: Riba prohibition eliminates the compounding engine, Zakat taxes idle offshore wealth, Amanah constrains elite betrayal, and Khalifah stewardship reframes resource ownership. The Prophet's Market of Medina — rent-free, no hoarding, no manipulation — is the antithesis of offshore financial centers. The question is whether modern societies can implement these mechanisms before the extraction game reaches its endgame of collapse.
