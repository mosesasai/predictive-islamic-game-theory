## 1. Current Problem Definition

**Proximity Rule:** Internal civil conflicts within nations override external geopolitical conflicts. Players optimize for the nearest game. This explains why leaders are assassinated by internal betrayal more often than external enemies, and why the US elite civil war (Democrats vs. MAGA), Israeli religious/secular schism, and Iranian theocrat/nationalist split dominate over external conflicts.

---

## 2. Classical Game Theory Approach

**Multi-Level Nested Games:** Each player simultaneously participates in internal (proximity) and external (geopolitical) games. Nash logic predicts that players prioritize the nearest game because:
- Internal rivals can hurt you faster than external enemies
- Internal defection has immediate consequences; external threats are discounted by distance/time
- The discount factor for nearby threats is higher: $\delta_{\text{internal}} > \delta_{\text{external}}$

**Nash Prediction:** All major geopolitical actors are primarily fighting internal battles. External conflicts are instruments of internal power games.

---

## 3. Islamic Game Theory Approach

1. **Ummah Unity as Counter-Proximity:** The Prophet's constitution created a supra-tribal identity that overrode the proximity rule. The Aws and Khazraj stopped fighting each other (nearest game) and united against external threats — because the Ummah identity made internal cooperation the dominant strategy.

2. **Munafiqun (Hypocrites) Management:** The Prophet acknowledged the proximity threat (internal betrayal by Munafiqun like Abdullah ibn Ubayy) but chose not to purge them — because executing internal traitors would validate the proximity rule and fracture the community. Instead, he managed them through patience and eventual marginalization.

3. **Concentric Loyalty Structure:** Islam establishes clear loyalty hierarchy: God → Prophet → Ummah → Family → Tribe. This overrides the "proximity rule" by anchoring loyalty to transcendent principles rather than immediate power dynamics.

## 5. Conclusion

The Law of Proximity is a powerful descriptive model: players do optimize for nearest games. But IGT demonstrates that this law can be overridden by a sufficiently strong supra-identity ($\alpha_{\text{Ummah}} > \alpha_{\text{tribe}}$) and transcendent loyalty structure ($\lambda \cdot \Omega(\text{Ummah loyalty}) > \text{material gain from internal defection}$). The Prophet's Medina is history's most successful case of overriding the proximity rule — transforming warring tribes into a unified community that faced external threats collectively. The key was not eliminating internal differences but creating a higher-order identity that made internal cooperation dominant.
