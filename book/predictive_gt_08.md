## 1. Current Problem Definition

**Communism as Controlled Opposition:** The speech argues that communism was a tool created by financial capitalists to destroy their real enemies — monarchy, religion, nationalism, and genuine democracy. The Russian Revolution was financed by Western banks and resulted in asset-stripping Russia for Western financial interests. The elite division is between financial capitalists (globalists) and nationalist sovereigntists.

---

## 2. Classical Game Theory Approach

**Elite Factional Game:**
- **Player A (Financial Capitalists):** Seek borderless capital flow, weak states, eliminated religion
- **Player B (Nationalist Sovereigntists):** Seek strong states, controlled borders, cultural traditions
- **Player C (Labor/People):** Seek fair wages, stability, community

**Nash Equilibrium:** A and B compete for control of C. Communism is A's strategy to co-opt C against B — destroying monarchy and nationalism while concentrating assets in A's hands. The working class (C) is a tool, never a beneficiary.

## 3. Islamic Game Theory Approach

1. **Third Way Economics:** Islam rejects both unchecked capitalism (A's game) and state ownership (B's game). The Zakat-Waqf-Mudaraba system creates a third economic model: private ownership with mandatory social obligation, voluntary redistribution, and risk-sharing enterprise.

2. **Anti-Manipulation:** Under IGT, using ideology to manipulate masses for elite gain is haram (violates Fard constraint). The akhirah cost of deceiving populations is $\Omega(\text{mass deception}) \ll 0$.

3. **Community Solidarity Without State Control:** The Prophet's Ummah provides social solidarity (addressing C's needs) without requiring either A's financial oligarchy or B's state authoritarianism. The mosque-market-madrasah triad provides community infrastructure outside both corporate and state control.

## 5. Conclusion

The communist specter was a Nash-optimal strategy for financial capitalists to eliminate competing power structures. IGT's integrated framework — where economics, governance, and spirituality are inseparable — resists the factional game because it cannot be co-opted by either A or B without violating its core principles.
